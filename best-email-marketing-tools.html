1. Mailchimp ‚Äî Best Overall for Beginners

Mailchimp is the name most people think of first when it comes to email marketing, and there's a good reason for that. It's been around since 2001 and has built a reputation for being user-friendly without sacrificing power.

What We Like

  • The drag-and-drop email editor is genuinely intuitive ‚Äî you can build a professional email in under 10 minutes.
  • The free plan allows up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails per month, which is plenty to get started.
  • Built-in audience insights help you understand who's engaging with your content.
  • Hundreds of pre-built templates that look clean and modern.

What to Keep in Mind

Mailchimp's pricing jumps once you exceed the free tier. The Standard plan starts at $13/month for 500 contacts but scales up quickly as your list grows. Some of the more advanced automation features are also locked behind higher-tier plans.

2. ConvertKit ‚Äî Best for Creators & Course Sellers

If you sell digital products, run a newsletter, or create content online, ConvertKit was essentially built for you. It takes a different approach to email marketing ‚Äî focusing on simplicity and visual automation flows rather than complex features you might never use.

What We Like

  • The visual automation builder is the cleanest we've seen. You can map out entire customer journeys with drag-and-drop ease.
  • Subscriber tagging makes it easy to segment your audience without creating separate lists.
  • The free plan covers up to 500 subscribers with unlimited broadcasts.
  • Emails look clean and professional with minimal design effort.

What to Keep in Mind

ConvertKit trades off some design flexibility for simplicity. If you need highly customized, visually elaborate emails, you might feel a bit limited. Also, the free plan doesn't include automation sequences ‚Äî you'll need the Creator plan ($9/month) for that.

3. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) ‚Äî Best Free Plan

Here's what makes Brevo stand out: it doesn't charge you based on how many contacts you have. Instead, you pay based on how many emails you send per month. For a growing business, this is a massive advantage.

What We Like

  • Unlimited contacts on every plan, including free. This is almost unheard of in the industry.
  • The free plan includes up to 300 emails per day ‚Äî enough for most small businesses.
  • Strong automation features even on lower-tier plans.
  • Built-in CRM, SMS marketing, and live chat make it a versatile all-in-one platform.

What to Keep in Mind

The email templates are solid but not quite as polished as Mailchimp's. The interface can feel a bit dense at first. If you're sending high volumes, the pricing for premium plans is competitive but worth comparing to alternatives.

4. Zoho Campaigns ‚Äî Best Value

Zoho Campaigns is a lesser-known name in this space, but it punches well above its weight in terms of features per dollar. It's especially attractive if you already use other Zoho products like their CRM.

What We Like

  • Extremely competitive pricing ‚Äî the Standard plan starts at just $3/month for up to 250 contacts.
  • Native integration with Zoho CRM means your sales and marketing data stays in one place.
  • A/B testing is available on all paid plans, which is rare at this price point.
  • Good reporting and analytics to track campaign performance.

What to Keep in Mind

Zoho's interface is functional but not as polished as Mailchimp or ConvertKit. If you're not already in the Zoho ecosystem, the learning curve is a bit steeper. Customer support can also be slower to respond compared to competitors.

5. ActiveCampaign ‚Äî Best for Advanced Automation

If you want serious marketing automation without enterprise-level complexity, ActiveCampaign is worth considering. It's particularly strong for businesses that want to create sophisticated customer journeys based on behavior and engagement.

What We Like

  • The automation builder is incredibly powerful ‚Äî you can create complex workflows that respond to almost any user action.
  • Built-in CRM that works seamlessly with your email campaigns.
  • Excellent deliverability rates, meaning your emails are more likely to land in the inbox.

What to Keep in Mind

There's no free plan, and the Lite plan starts at $9/month for 500 contacts. The platform has a steeper learning curve than Mailchimp or ConvertKit, so it's best suited for businesses that are ready to invest time in learning a more powerful tool.
